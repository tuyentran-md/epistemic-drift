# Type 1: Inference Drift

## Definition

AI generates conclusions that are statistically plausible but were never derived from the actual data through a controlled human reasoning process.

## Mechanism

1. Researcher provides raw data or preliminary findings to AI
2. AI produces an interpretation that is *coherent* and *consistent* with the numbers
3. The interpretation contains causal claims, mechanistic explanations, or contextual framing that the study design cannot support
4. Researcher accepts the interpretation because it "makes sense"

## Why it's dangerous

The output passes all standard checks:
- Statistics are correct ✓
- Conclusions align with data ✓
- Language is appropriately cautious ✓

But the *reasoning path* from data to conclusion was generated by a language model optimizing for coherence, not by a human applying domain expertise and methodological constraints.

## Detection signals

- Conclusions that are "too clean" — every finding neatly supports the narrative
- Mechanistic explanations that weren't part of the original study design
- Discussion sections that introduce causal language where the study is observational
- Implications that extend beyond what the data can support, but do so elegantly

## Field-specific manifestations

### Clinical research
AI interprets surgical outcomes data and suggests that technique A is "associated with improved recovery" — language that implies causation in an observational study.

### Social science
AI produces interpretations of survey data that weave together psychological mechanisms the researchers never hypothesized.

### Basic science
AI explains experimental results using molecular pathways that are plausible but were not tested in the experiment.

## Countermeasures

1. **Pre-register interpretive frameworks** — Specify in advance what conclusions the study can and cannot support
2. **Separate data analysis from interpretation** — Use AI for computation, not for meaning-making
3. **Track reasoning provenance** — Document which interpretations came from the researcher vs. the AI
