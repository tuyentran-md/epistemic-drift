# Type 3: Narrative Drift

## Definition

AI constructs a compelling story connecting findings in ways the researcher did not originally conceive, and the researcher adopts this narrative without recognizing it as AI-generated framing.

## Mechanism

1. Researcher has a set of findings — some strong, some weak, some unexpected
2. AI is asked to help write the discussion or frame the implications
3. AI weaves everything into a coherent, persuasive narrative
4. The narrative becomes the paper's identity — the researcher internalizes it as their own thinking

## The "too-clean" problem

Real research is messy. Findings contradict each other. Some results don't fit. Limitations are real and uncomfortable.

AI-generated narratives smooth over this messiness. Every finding supports the conclusion. Every limitation is elegantly addressed. Every implication flows naturally. This is not what genuine research looks like — it's what *marketing* looks like.

## Why researchers adopt AI narratives

- The narrative is better than what they could write themselves
- It feels like their own thinking, just "better expressed"
- There's no clear moment where they can point to and say "the AI made this up"
- Academic incentives reward compelling narratives

## Detection signals

- Discussion sections that are suspiciously well-organized
- Every limitation has a silver lining
- Implications section reads like a research proposal for future funding
- The paper tells a "complete story" with no loose ends
- Cited references in the discussion that the researcher hasn't actually read (AI-suggested citations)

## The ghostwriting distinction

Traditional ghostwriting: Human writes, author signs. The reasoning is human.

AI narrative drift: AI generates the *reasoning framework* — the way findings relate to each other, the story they tell, the implications they carry. The author doesn't just sign someone else's writing; they adopt someone else's *thinking*.

## Countermeasures

1. **Write the discussion BEFORE using AI** — Draft your own interpretation first, then compare
2. **Flag AI-generated framing** — Mark sections where AI suggested connections you hadn't considered
3. **Adversarial reading** — Ask: "Would I have written this exact argument without AI?"
